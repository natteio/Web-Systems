For a centralized bank the transaction would take about a week for it to be fully processed, and in terms of an emergency transaction, this wouldn’t be a great case, while the blockchain technology provides the total opposite. Being decentralized, the transaction would only take about minutes to be fully processed. For the case of bitcoin, it’s the case of lower fraud risks. 2.     Find the information security policy at your place of employment or study. Is it a good policy? Does it meet the standards outlined in the chapter?

 

I would say that our current study has at least 2 information security policy taking place, even though we really didn’t written a security policy but nevertheless, we’ve been practicing certain policies. Firstly, password protection policy. We believed that password is an important aspect of security, so a very easy password would likely to result in an unauthorized access to pieces or bulk of information. In the case of our study, we implement a hashing algorithm for our end-users passwords. Secondly, Remote Access Policy. We are forbidden to access the server files and database remotely, mainly because although our local network is owned by us, it does not complete assure that we are the only one connecting to it – it could be friends, family and even other people. It is said the remote access can boost productivity, however, the risk would be with the system becoming more vulnerable to attacks, in which this is not a good case, so by practicing this policy, it surely does minimizes the system from being exposed to ground breaking damages.
